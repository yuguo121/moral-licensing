# Project Overview: Moral Licensing in ESG and Earnings Management

This directory contains research materials for a scholarly project investigating the relationship between Corporate ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) engagement and Earnings Management through the lens of **Moral Licensing**.

The research explores how substantive ESG efforts (particularly Environmental) might provide a "license" for subsequent ethically questionable behavior, such as earnings management, and how this effect is moderated by **industry culpability** (distinguishing between "progress framing" and "commitment framing").

## Key Files

- **`Corporate ESG Engagement and Earnings Management_v3.docx`**: The primary manuscript for the research paper. It contains the refined theorizing, methodology, results, and discussion.
- **`Communication.txt`**: A record of key updates and methodological defenses communicated to Prof. He. It highlights:
    - The use of **Entropy Balancing** to address selection bias.
    - **Lead-lag tests** to rule out the "insurance hypothesis."
    - Differentiation between **Environmental effects** (substantive sacrifice) and **Social effects** (instrumental logic).
- **`GEMINI.md`**: (This file) Provides instructional context and a high-level overview of the directory for AI-assisted research and editing.

## Research Context & Methodology

- **Theoretical Framework**: Moral Licensing theory, specifically focusing on how industry culpability impacts whether ESG is framed as "progress" (triggering licensing) or "commitment" (constraining licensing).
- **Key Variables**: Corporate ESG Engagement (Independent), Earnings Management (Dependent), Industry Culpability (Moderator).
- **Methodological Highlights**:
    - **Entropy Balancing**: Rigorous selection bias correction.
    - **Mechanism Validation**: Contrasting Environmental vs. Social dimensions of ESG to strengthen theoretical contributions.
