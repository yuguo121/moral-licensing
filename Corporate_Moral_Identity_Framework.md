# Level of Analysis & Corporate Moral Identity Framework

This document defines the elegant "Firm-level" theoretical framework for the study, resolving the tension between "Moral Licensing" and "Moral Consistency."

## 1. Level of Analysis: The Firm as a Moral Entity
To ensure alignment with the data (ESG scores, Earnings Management, Industry Culpability), the theory is constructed at the **Firm Level** rather than the individual/TMT level.

- **Corporate Moral Identity:** The organization is treated as an entity with its own moral self-concept and reputation.
- **Elegance:** Firm-level reasoning avoids the "level-of-analysis gap" (the ecological fallacy) that occurs when predicting organizational outcomes from unobserved personal psychological states.

## 2. The "Moral Asset" vs. "Moral Identity" Pivot
The "Moral Licensing" effect is determined by how the firm's moral credit is interpreted within its organizational logic.

| Dimension | **ESG as a "Moral Asset"** | **ESG as a "Moral Identity"** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Interpretation** | A resource/buffer to be "spent" or traded. | A core value to be maintained and lived up to. |
| **Psychological Path** | **Progress Framing:** "We have done enough." | **Commitment Framing:** "This is who we are." |
| **Behavioral Outcome** | **Moral Licensing:** Entitlement to manage earnings (EM $\uparrow$). | **Moral Consistency:** Constraint on opportunistic behavior (EM $\downarrow$). |

## 3. High-Order Moderator: Moral Identity Centrality
"Industry Culpability" serves as a primary proxy for the **Centrality of ESG to the firm's identity**:

- **Culpable (Dirty) Industries:** ESG is **Central**. Survival depends on moral transformation. The firm adopts a **Moral Identity** logic $\rightarrow$ **Consistency** (Lower EM).
- **Non-Culpable (Clean) Industries:** ESG is **Peripheral**. It is viewed as "extra credit" or a reputation-building **Asset** $\rightarrow$ **Licensing** (Higher EM).

## 4. Theoretical "Wrap-up" for UTD24
The study moves beyond "if" ESG affects financial reporting to **"when" and "why"** the same moral action leads to opposing outcomes. The elegance lies in the symmetry of the "Asset vs. Identity" framework, which can be further validated using firm-level proxies like **ESG History Consistency** and **Governance Structures**.
